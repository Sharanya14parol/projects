the Guest Book

by Sarah Blake

Description

"The Guest Book" by Sarah Blake is a multigenerational saga that delves into the complexities of family secrets, societal expectations, and the enduring impacts of privilege and prejudice. Set against the backdrop of an old-money family's summer retreat on an island in Maine, the novel spans several decades, beginning in the 1930s and moving through to the present day
Sarah Blake's prose is both evocative and insightful, bringing to life the idyllic yet insular world of the Miltons. Through richly drawn characters and a narrative that weaves together past and present, "The Guest Book" offers a poignant reflection on the ways in which history shapes and haunts the present

About the Author

Sarah Blake is an American writer based in Washington D.C. Her debut novel Grange House, set in Victorian era Maine, was published in 2001. Her second, The Postmistress, a story set in Second World War Massachusetts and London, was published in 2010, and a third, The Guest Book, the story of two intertwined families in twentieth-century Germany and the U.S., appeared in 2019
