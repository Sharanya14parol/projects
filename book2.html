Ikigai

by Carcia and Franecsc miralles

Description

Ikigai is a Japanese concept that combines the terms "iki," meaning life, and "gai," meaning worth. It is often translated to "a reason for being" and signifies the source of value in one's life or the things that make life worthwhile.
In "Ikigai: The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life," authors Héctor García and Francesc Miralles delve into the Japanese tradition of ikigai, exploring how it contributes to a fulfilling and meaningful existence. The book introduces readers to the lifestyle and secrets of the residents of Okinawa, a region in Japan noted for its high number of centenarians and vibrant, healthy elderly population. Through a blend of interviews, scientific research, and personal anecdotes, García and Miralles uncover the key elements of ikigai. They explore how purpose, passion, and daily routines contribute to longevity and happiness. The book provides practical advice on how readers can discover their own ikigai, offering tools to find balance, reduce stress, and enhance well-being

About the Author

Héctor García and Francesc Miralles are the coauthors of The Book of Ichigo Ichie and The Four-Way Path. Héctor is a citizen of Japan, where he has lived for over a decade, and is the author of A Geek in Japan, a #1 bestseller in Japan. Francesc is the author of a number of bestselling self-help and inspirational books and of the novel Love in Lowercase, which has been translated into twenty languages.
